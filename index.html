Under a fully representative and just electoral system, the percent of the population living in a particular state should match that percentage of electoral votes that state has. Alas, this is not the case and has not been the case throughout the entire XXth century. Choose different census years in the bottom left slider and hover with your mouse to see the percentage share of electoral votes as a fraction of a states percent population.

Data sources:

US Electoral votes per state: Kaggle dataset

US population by state by census: Iowa Data Center

US Census apportionement and representatives: Census.gov
